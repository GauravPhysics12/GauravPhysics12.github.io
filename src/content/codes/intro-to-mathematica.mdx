**Mathematica is an incredibly powerful tool for physicists. While simple calculations are easy, its true strength lies in solving the complex equations that describe the universe. This guide expands on the basics to cover essential physics applications: Calculus, Differential Equations, and Vector Algebra.**

**Note:** The structure and content for these tutorials are inspired by the methods presented in **Sadri Hassani's *Mathematical Methods for Students of Physics and Related Fields***.

### Downloadable Notebook Tutorials

You can download the full, commented Mathematica notebooks used in this tutorial below. You will need Wolfram Mathematica or the free Wolfram Player to view them.

* **[Introduction to Mathematica](/images/mathematica/Ch1-Intro-to-Mathematica.nb)**
  * **Description:** A beginner-friendly introduction covering the fundamental commands, syntax, and basic plotting techniques. Start here if you are new to Mathematica.

* **[Vectors and Matrices](/images/mathematica/Vec-Mat.nb)**
  * **Description:** A tutorial on essential linear algebra operations, including dot products, cross products.

* **[Integration and Calculus](/images/mathematica/Int.nb)**
  * **Description:** A deep dive into using Mathematica for both definite and indefinite integration, crucial for calculating quantities like Work and potential energy.
