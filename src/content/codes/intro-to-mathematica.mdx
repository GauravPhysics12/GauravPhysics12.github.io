Mathematica is an incredibly powerful tool for physicists. While simple calculations are easy, its true strength lies in solving the complex equations that describe the universe. This guide expands on the basics to cover essential physics applications: Calculus, Differential Equations, and Vector Algebra.

*Note: The structure and content for these tutorials are inspired by the methods presented in Sadri Hassani's "Mathematical Methods for Students of Physics and Related Fields".*
